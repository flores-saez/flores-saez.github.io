01 — About

I am Alonso
Flores-Sáez

I study digital culture and its effects on democratic processes — using data to understand how communities form identity in networked environments.

My research interests center on social networks, demographic analysis, and the intersection of technology and civic life. My graduate work and master's thesis examined digital technology use and perception of community identification among young adults in the U.S.

I have technical expertise in R, STATA, SPSS, NVivo, and ArcGIS. My work informs resource and policy development, strategic advocacy, and collaborative research for organizations working on disability, education, and free expression. I am bilingual in English and Spanish, and fluent in French.
